## Key Ideas:

-   Realtime FaceSoftening Approach to smooth out blemishes in the
    facial skin region, followed by a wavelet band manipulation to
    restore the underlying skin texture.
-   Guided Feathering to preserve hair-like regions within the face (
    beard, mustache)
-   Uses wavelet image decompositon module to regain texture of the
    smoothened images.

    ![](/assets/img/fabsoften-pipeline.png "Pipeline"){: .ci}


## What did the Authors tried to accomplish?

-   Blemish removal while retaining facial feature sharpness
-   restoring sking texture as well as fine hair texture
-   Handling Varied light conditions.

## Proposed Method

-   Initial preprocessing to extract the face and remove the large
    blemishes

    -   detect the faces present in the image and their corresponding 68
        facial landmark points on the facial feature using cubic curves
        to obtain an approximate outer countour of these facial
        fearures.
    -   To generate a binary skin mask, fill skin regions with ones and
        non-skin regions with zeros.
    -   To detect the blemishes, Canny Edge Detector to localize strong
        edge patterns.
    -   Use DFS (depth first traversal ) from these connected edges that
        are likely to represent blemish boundaries.
    -   If a loop is fromed, then it is considered a large blemish.
        (spots) Further prune, using edge magnitude and shape
        information to reject weaker ones.
    -   Finally Concealing, detected spots by replicating pixel values
        from their neighboring blemish free skin patches.



-   Skin Mask Generation
    -   Inspired by Guided Filtering, use the output of preprocessing
        part as a guide to refine the skin-probability mask.
    -   Skin probability mask is computed using this method 
        [here](https://projet.liris.cnrs.fr/imagine/pub/proceedings/ICIP-2009/pdfs/0003149.pdf).

![](/assets/img/fabsoften-skin-mask.png "Results"){: .ci}

-   Skin Imperfection Smoothing

    -   Appropiate smoothing filter that preserves facial features like
        eyes, lip-lines.
    -   Experiment with different filters.
    -   Use the ADF (Attribute aware dynamic guided filter. ) which it
        the modified version of guided filter.

        ![](/assets/img/fabsoften-texture.png "Results"){: .ci}

    

-   Skin Texture Generation

    -   Use of Wavelet domain image decomposition as it effectively
        separates low-frequency and high-frequency components of the
        image.
    -   After applying smmothing filter, they clean the essential
        details, fine-grained textures in skin regions.
    -   In summary, the power of the STR module comes from taking more
        information from the lower frequency levels of Sw and less
        information from the lower frequency levels of Iw to obtain Ow
        and vice versa for high-frequency bands. This results in a
        fusion of the uniform skin region in the filtered output S
        (which are represented by low-frequency lev- els) with the
        texture present in the input image I (which is represented by
        high-frequency levels). This fusion creates a filtered output S,
        where the facial nonuniformities present in low-frequency bands
        of the original image are smoothed out while the skin texture
        present in high-frequency bands is retained. ( I is input image,
        and S is smoothened image)
